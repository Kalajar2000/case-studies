Functional Mushroom Supplement

Advertorial creative for a category where nearly every competitor advertises the same ingredient list. The differentiator chosen was extraction quality — a specification most brands bury in the FAQ — and the mechanism of the product became the story.

Advertorial was the right format because the argument needs room. A 15-second video cannot explain why extraction ratio changes what the customer actually absorbs; a long-form editorial can, and it gives the ad something to say that a competitor cannot copy inside a week.

Why it worked: in a commoditised category, the specification is the angle. Whoever explains the mechanism first owns it.
Shown: advertorial opener. The headline commits to a mechanism claim and sets a reading-time expectation before the pitch.

Nootropic Focus Drink

Shown: comparison static built to be legible at thumbnail size in-feed, with the numbers doing the persuading.

Habit change

Quit-Nicotine Brand

UGC and social-proof creative. Testimonial-led throughout, because in habit change the only voice a sceptical buyer finds credible is somebody who has already done the thing.

The creative overlays a real comment onto the testimonial itself, so the proof and the story arrive in the same frame rather than asking the viewer to go looking for reviews.

The angle: social proof does the persuading and the brand stays out of the way. Scepticism-then-conversion is the arc that performs.
Shown: testimonial with an overlaid comment card. "I thought it was a joke at first" is the scepticism beat, stated by the customer rather than pre-empted by the brand.

How I work

  1. Map awareness and sophistication. Where the market already sits decides whether the ad opens with a problem, a mechanism, or a claim. Skipping this step is the single most common reason a creative test produces unreadable results.
  2. Write hooks per angle before anything is designed. The angle is the unit of testing, not the asset. Design follows the message, never the reverse.
  3. Run statics and UGC video in parallel. Same angles, two formats. This separates the winning message from the winning format, which a single-format test cannot do.
  4. Brief editors and creators so they execute once. A brief that needs three rounds of clarification was not finished when it was sent. Briefs go out with the hook, the beat structure, the references and the reason.
  5. Iterate on hook rate, hold rate, CTR and CPC using a 3:2:2 structure, and kill on evidence rather than on taste.
What I don't do: I don't edit video and I don't buy media. I hand finished briefs, hooks and scripts to your editors, and the numbers back to whoever runs the account. If you need one person to do all three, I'm the wrong hire and I'd rather say so now.
